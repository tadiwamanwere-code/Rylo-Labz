01 — The problem

Africa's education system
is in crisis.

70% of Zimbabwean students fail their O-Levels. The country is short more than 50,000 teachers — and loses 4,000 more every year to emigration. Parents pay premium fees for average outcomes. Universities receive under-prepared intake, year after year.

This isn't a content problem. It's an infrastructure problem. The teachers, time, and one-to-one attention required to close the gap simply do not exist — and no amount of traditional hiring will produce them fast enough.

We think AI changes the shape of what's possible here. Not as a replacement for teachers, but as the infrastructure that makes every teacher more effective and every student individually taught.
